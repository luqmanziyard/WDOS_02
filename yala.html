Yala

Tales of Yala

The region of Yala is home to the popular wildlife park and bird sanctuary – the Yala National Park, Sri Lanka. It is a great place to enjoy the rustic outdoors, to embark on exciting jungle safaris, to explore shipwrecks and to experience the rich culture and history of the diverse island of Sri Lanka.

Attraction in Yala

The main attractions in the region of Yala are the Yala National Park and the Kumana Bird Sanctuary. Both parks are great places to experience majestic animals such as the Sri Lankan elephant, leopard and sloth bear, in addition to a wide variety of reptiles, birds and other mammals. Yala is the ideal place to be based in if you want to enjoy visits to cultural sites such as the Sithulpawwa Raja Maha Viharaya, and the Kataragama Devalaya.

Experience in Yala

Experience Sri Lanka’s diverse wildlife in a glorious way when you visit the national parks in Yala. Be blown away by sightings of the majestic Sri Lankan elephant and elusive Sri Lankan leopard, in addition to many endemic species of birds. Enjoy the bush experience by camping out in nature, or go the luxury route of glamping. Head over to Kataragama to have a wonderful experience of the country’s multiculturalism, or venture deep into the Yala Park to visit the Sithulpawwa Raja Maha Viharaya and experience an important historic site in the region.
