ABOUT

Sri Lanka may be a small island, but every inch of it is packed with stunning landscapes, sandy beaches, clear blue waters, ancient ruins, heritage sites, wildlife reserves and natural rainforests. The compact size of the island means that you could be snorkelling on the west coast one day, tracking leopards in the southeast the next day, and hiking in the cold misty mountains the following day. The country’s easily accessible and varied landscape lets you experience more during your holiday herhere. Furthermore, Sri Lanka has generally great weather all year-round, so you can visit the country at any time of year. However, take note that due to the monsoon rains, the west and south of the country is more suitable from November to April, while the east and north east are more suitable for travel from May to October.

Yala

In the present day, Yala is a popular wildlife reserve that is home to a wide variety of animal, bird, reptile and plant life. However, the region of Yala once belonged to a thriving ancient kingdom – the Kingdom of Ruhuna. It is also believed to have been home to a species of ancient beings, as well as a tribe of indigenous people.

For some undiscovered reason, the territory was abandoned by humans and became overrun with the wilderness possibly after the 13th century. Discover the past of Yala as you explore the tales that surround this popular destination

Sigiriya

The Sigiriya Rock Fortress has a dramatic and thrilling past that is bound to captivate all who visit the site. Towering over its surroundings, this ancient fortress is a fitting background for a tale of epic proportions.

Discover a tale of ambition, betrayal and vengeance when you learn about the king who had the vision and perseverance to create this magnificent palace in the sky.

Galle Fort

The city of Galle has a history that predates the Christian era, when it was a popular trading port used by Indian, Arab and Chinese merchants. The presence of so many varied nationalities resulted in the early city being a multicultural haven. The significance of the port of Galle in the shipping routes of the Indian ocean meant that it attracted the attention of the European colonizers who arrived in Sri Lanka during the 16th century.

Galle became an important strategic location for the Portug guese, Dutch and British empires, and it continued to develop as a city during the colonial period. The tales that surround the city of Galle and its borders range from the mythical to historical. Check out the tales featured here to learn more about the fascinating past of this charming coastal town.
